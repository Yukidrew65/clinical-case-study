Clinical intake & symptom triage copilot

Lead UX Designer — Yukifumi Date — May 2026 Duration — 10–14 weeks
Project summary

A dual-surface AI copilot for primary care. The patient side helps people articulate symptoms clearly before a GP appointment — reducing the anxiety, vagueness, and clinical friction that come with self-reporting. The clinician side converts that input into a structured, triaged summary with automatic red-flag signal detection.

This sits at the intersection of conversational AI, clinical safety, and NHS-aligned service design.

The problem space

Patient side

Patients arrive underprepared — unsure how to describe symptoms, anxious about wasting a GP's time, and prone to either over-reporting or omitting critical details. Pre-appointment anxiety compounds recall errors.

Clinician side

GPs receive fragmented, inconsistent histories. Critical symptoms are buried in noise. Time per consultation is shrinking. There is no structured intake layer between patient registration and the appointment itself.

Design objectives

  • Build a conversational intake flow that guides patients without leading them — maintaining clinical accuracy while reducing pre-appointment anxiety.

  • Design a clinician-facing summary view that flags red-flag signals clearly, using established triage frameworks (Manchester Triage System).

  • Define appropriate AI boundaries — what the tool surfaces vs what it must not attempt to diagnose or advise.

  • Create an interaction model that accounts for health literacy variance, digital access barriers, and emotional state at point of use.

  • Ensure design decisions are grounded in NHS design principles, MHRA guidance, and existing GP workflow constraints.

Research plan
01

Secondary research — 2 weeks

Open NHS datasets (NHS Digital), Manchester Triage System protocols, published literature on pre-appointment anxiety and digital health intake. Map the regulatory context (MHRA, NICE, NHS AI Lab guidance). Review competitor products: Limbic, Bupa's digital health suite, Flo Health.

02

Patient interviews — 5 to 8 participants

Semi-structured. Focus on pre-appointment experience, symptom articulation anxiety, trust in AI health tools, and past encounters where underprepared communication led to poor outcomes. Recruit across age range and health literacy levels.

03

GP advisor sessions — 1 to 2 clinicians

Identify via LinkedIn (NHS GP registrars or digital health-engaged GPs). Validate red-flag taxonomy, understand information they most need upfront, and stress-test the clinician summary design against real workflow pressures.

04

Synthesis and framing

Jobs-to-be-done mapping, affinity clustering, clinical risk taxonomy, and a dual-persona model (patient + clinician). Define what the AI can and cannot do within safe, regulated boundaries.

Design deliverables

Patient-facing surface

Conversational intake flow, symptom articulation prompts, anxiety-aware interaction model, accessibility-first UI. Progress indicator, edit-back functionality, and clear AI disclosure language.

Clinician-facing surface

Structured intake summary, red-flag signal layer (severity-coded), timeline view of symptom onset, and one-click escalation paths. Designed around a 10-minute GP consultation window.

Design system components

Clinical card patterns, triage severity indicators, safe AI disclosure components, and a reusable conversational UI kit aligned to NHS design principles.

Research documentation

Interview analysis report, clinical risk taxonomy, regulatory constraint map, and a decision log documenting every key design tradeoff and its evidence basis.
